## Preamble

Vision & Mission defines what Guild CMS is trying to become, who it exists to serve, and how the project should measure long-term success.

Guild CMS exists because long-running gaming communities need software that respects their history, adapts to their future, and remains maintainable by the people who inherit it.

## Vision Statement

Guild CMS shall become a secure, modular, extensible content management platform designed for guilds, clans, alliances, gaming communities, and long-running online organizations.

## Mission Statement

The mission of Guild CMS is to provide a practical platform for building, managing, and extending community websites while keeping administration approachable and engineering standards strong.

## Audience and Community Role

Guild CMS exists for gaming communities, developers, administrators, community officers, archivists, and technical stewards who need to operate, customize, extend, or preserve community infrastructure.

## Product Direction

Guild CMS is moving toward a modular CMS core with documented extension points, a predictable installation and upgrade path, a theme engine, provider interfaces, plugins, APIs, and developer tools.

## Engineering Direction

The project prioritizes security, maintainability, architecture governance, incremental release discipline, and documentation as part of the product.

## Documentation Direction

The Engineering Library is the authoritative home for public engineering knowledge. The Development Center tracks engineering state and links to the public publications rather than duplicating their content.

## Success Criteria

Guild CMS succeeds when it is usable by administrators, extensible by developers, maintainable by future contributors, and trusted by the communities that depend on it.

## Stewardship

Guild CMS should be built for continuity across contributors, servers, communities, technology changes, and operational transitions.
