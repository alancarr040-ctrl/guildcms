# GCMS-ENG-011 — User Experience & Educational Design Principles

**Publication:** Publication 11  
**Volume:** Volume XI  
**Status:** Published  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Phase:** 4.4.0-2  
**Last Updated:** June 2026  
**Applies To:** Guild CMS v0.9+

## Preamble

Guild CMS exists to help people succeed, not merely to complete tasks. A site owner, administrator, contributor, or developer should leave each Guild CMS interaction with more confidence than they had when they arrived.

This publication establishes the product experience principles that guide the installer, Administration Center, public documentation, plugin workflows, upgrade tools, and future user-facing interfaces.

## Principles

1. **Explain before asking.** Guild CMS should explain what a screen is doing before it asks someone to provide information.
2. **Teach, do not assume.** The software should teach the basics at the moment the information is needed.
3. **The installer is the first teacher.** Installation should introduce the architecture gently and build confidence.
4. **Confidence is a product goal.** Successful steps should confirm what happened; risky steps should explain risk before action.
5. **Errors should educate.** Error messages should explain what happened, why it likely happened, what was protected, and what to do next.
6. **Documentation complements the interface.** Documentation teaches mastery; the interface teaches the immediate task.
7. **Use role-based language.** Prefer site owner, administrator, developer, contributor, maintainer, and visitor when those roles improve clarity.
8. **Use progressive disclosure.** Provide essential guidance directly and deeper explanation nearby.
9. **Design accessibly and respectfully.** Friendly language must remain clear, accessible, and never patronizing.
10. **Review the experience.** Every user-facing change should be evaluated for clarity, confidence, recovery, and educational value.

## Installer Guidance

Every installer step should answer:

- What is this?
- Why is Guild CMS asking for it?
- Where does the site owner find this information?
- What happens next?
- What happens if something goes wrong?

The installer should be enough for a first-time site owner to reach a working Guild CMS installation without being forced to leave the process and search documentation for ordinary steps.

## Certification

GCMS-ENG-011 is published as the first Engineering Library publication focused on product experience and educational design. It applies to installer work beginning in Phase 4.4 and to future administration, plugin, theme, upgrade, and documentation workflows.
